Copyright and Privacy

Research and discuss how copyright and rivacy affects you as a web developer

Copyright

Copyright plays a big role in the web development industry. It stops people from using your content that you have created without your permission and rules about how to go about using your content or stop other people using your content for commercial use and make money off of the content. The New Zealand copyright Act 1994 says you do not even need to put copyright symbols on content you have created or even register the work for it to be copyrighted. (“Ownership of copyright • Copyright Licensing,” n.d.). If you use copyright material for educational purposes, you must credit the author and where you got the content from. Work is not automatically protected with international copyright law throughout the whole world but only to the countries that agree to it. (“International Copyright Law and Legal Definition | USLegal, Inc.,” n.d.) Creative Commons works off of the base of Copyright to give creators more flexibility and choices and gives them simple and standardized way to grant copyright permissions to their work. (“About The Licenses—Creative Commons,” n.d.)

Privacy

Websites collect a lot of user personal information be it sign up forms or contact forms and because of this we need to keep our client's data secure. New Zealand has a Privacy Act that protects the people of New Zealand, the act consists of 12 Principles:

  1. Purpose of collection of personal information.
  2. Source of personal information.
  3. Collection of information from subjects.
  4. Manner of collection of personal information.
  5. Storage and security of personal information.
  6. Access to personal information.
  7. Correction of personal information.
  8. Accuracy, etc., of personal information to be checked before use.
  9. Agency not to keep personal information for longer than necessary.
  10. Limits on use of personal information.
  11. Limits on disclosure of personal information.
  12. Unique identifiers.
(“What is personal information and the Privacy Act? - Data.govt.nz,” n.d.)

We as developers of the websites must make sure we follow these principles to the tee.
